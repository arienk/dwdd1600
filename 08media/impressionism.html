Impressionism:

The impressionist style was first developed in France. Impressionism was not liked as an art style at first. This is because it rejected the academic standards, therefore gaining lots of criticism from art critics. There was a group of women that critic Henri Focillon called “Les Trois Grandes Dames'' meaning “The Three Great Ladies.” The title Impressionism actually derived from a review given by a critic that said the art had looked like impressions, the art wasn’t finished. At the time, impressionist artists referred to themselves as the Independents.

Elements-

  1. Wide range of colors
  2. En plein air (painting outdoors)
  3. Obvious brushstrokes
  4. Strong use of light
  5. Texture
  6. Rejection of 3D perspective
  7. Landscape
